Frequently Asked Questions

What is JuneJams about?

JuneJams is a 2-day codecamp which encompasses programming workshops, fireside chats with current NUS Computing students, sharing sessions with external organisations and inspiring talks from accomplished women in the STEM field.

How do I get there?

JuneJams will be held at COM1 Seminar Room 1, The National University of Singapore. Its 5 minutes away from Opp Kent Ridge MRT by the NUS shuttle bus service D2.

What if I don't have a team?

Don't worry! We will host a team formation event at the beginning of the codecamp so everyone will have a team.

Do I need to pay?

No need! JuneJams is free of charge! :)

What if I have no coding experience?

You don't need to know how to code! We have range of workshops during the codecamp that will introduce you to programming and tech!

Will food be provided?

Lunch will be provided! However, if you feel that you may be hungry, you may also bring some snacks!

What do I need to bring?

Your own laptops and most importantly, your willingness to learn!

Why JuneJams?

JuneJams provides a safe space for girls to dip their toes into the tech world, allowing them to figure out if it's their forte. We understanding that coding may deemed as a "guy thing", but we would like to change that notion!

Who can attend?

You are eligible to attend if you're a female secondary school student!

Why only girls?

We would like to encourage women to pursue STEM by providing a safe space to explore, acquire skills, and gain inspiration from female role models.

Additionally, we want to challenge societal stereotypes, especially those surrounding gender-related career choices.

What if I cannot attend both days?

Preferably, it would be good if you could attend both days! However, in the unlikely event you are unable to, just be sure to indicate it in the signup form with a valid reason.

You can also take a look at the resources provided to catch up with what you have missed out!

Have some other questions?

You can email us to any questions you still have.
